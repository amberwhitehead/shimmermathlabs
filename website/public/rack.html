01Warning Signs

You may be nerd-sniped if:
  • Sudden inability to continue the conversation.
  • Eyes glazing over; reaching for a napkin.
  • Muttering about edge cases.
  • Stopping mid-sentence to evaluate a series.
  • Stopping traffic over a resistor grid. (cf. xkcd #356)

02Rules of Engagement

  1. Agree on your axioms before the scene, not during.
  2. Settle the Continuum Hypothesis upfront.
  3. Establish a safeword. Recommended: Halt.
  4. Check in: “Does this definition feel okay?”
  5. No non-consensual theorem-dropping in casual conversation.

04Hard & Soft Limits

Hard Limits

  • The Axiom of Choice without prior warning.
  • “Assume there is a proof...”
  • Imprecise analogies connecting two subfields of math.
  • The Simulation Hypothesis.
  • Any “math” puzzle from Facebook.

Soft Limits (with negotiation)

  • Banach–Tarski paradox.
  • The Continuum Hypothesis.
  • Church-Turing Thesis.
  • The Riemann Hypothesis (long scenes only).
  • The Exponential Time Hypothesis (cryptographic bondage play only).
  • Mentioning Grothendieck.
  • Posting paper links in chat.
  • Anything ending in “…and then you can prove it in one line.”

06The Drop — Aftercare

Math drop is real.

After an intense session with an open problem you may feel: empty, haunted, unable to stop thinking about it, or compelled to look it up at 3am.

Aftercare: a hot beverage, a gentle trivial result (e.g. 1+1=2 in Peano arithmetic), cuddles, and sleep.
